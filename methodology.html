Methodology

How Front List Intel Works

Front List Intel combines objective market signals with disciplined editorial analysis to identify meaningful publishing opportunities before they become widely recognized.

A disciplined discovery process

Front List Intel does not attempt to predict bestsellers. It identifies books already demonstrating meaningful market activity and evaluates whether those titles may represent opportunities for publishing professionals.

Every stage systematically removes noise until only a small set of meaningful publishing opportunities remains.

Phase 1

Market Collection

At the beginning of each week, Front List Intel surveys more than 800 newly released titles across multiple publishing categories—from Biography & Memoir to Science Fiction & Fantasy—to establish the initial universe for analysis.

Phase 2

Objective Screening

The initial universe undergoes a consistent, rules-based screening process designed to remove obvious non-candidates. This process narrows the field to titles that may represent meaningful publishing opportunities.

Phase 3

Editorial Review

The remaining candidates undergo a publishing-focused editorial review designed to identify authors and titles that align with Front List Intel's scope and mission. This stage considers an author's commercial profile, body of work, market relevance, and potential publishing opportunity. The objective is to focus additional research on the most promising candidates while significantly reducing the candidate pool and keeping the focus on emerging opportunity rather than popularity alone.

Phase 4

Metadata Collection

The remaining candidates undergo a deeper level of research designed to build a more complete commercial profile for each title.

Additional metadata—including publication details, format availability, pricing, reader engagement, and other market indicators—is collected to provide the context necessary for evaluating potential publishing opportunities.

Phase 5

Opportunity Assessment

The remaining candidates undergo a final evaluation to identify meaningful publishing opportunities.

This assessment considers the complete commercial profile developed during earlier phases and identifies signals that may be relevant to publishing professionals, including format opportunities, publishing models, and other indicators of commercial opportunity.

By this stage, the initial universe has been reduced to a small number of titles that demonstrate meaningful publishing relevance and potential opportunity.

Ongoing Tracking

Momentum Framework

Titles selected for the Front List continue to be monitored using a proprietary momentum framework.

Momentum Score considers current Kindle Store Rank, week-over-week Kindle Store Rank movement, and week-over-week ratings growth. The exact methodology and weighting are proprietary and intentionally not published.

Editorial curation

The Front List is not simply a ranked list. Featured selections are chosen to represent different kinds of publishing opportunities, including new arrivals, standout debuts, breakout titles, and other emerging market signals.

The objective is to help publishing professionals focus their attention earlier, before a title's momentum becomes obvious to the broader market.

Long-term validation

Front List Intel also maintains historical bestseller data to evaluate how emerging titles develop over time and to measure the effectiveness of its methodology.

As the archive grows, Front List Intel will be able to identify titles that first appeared on the Front List before later reaching major bestseller lists.
