Methodology

How Front List Intel Works

Front List Intel combines objective market signals with disciplined editorial analysis to identify titles where meaningful market momentum intersects with publishing opportunity.

Our Approach

Momentum alone is not enough.

Front List Intel does not attempt to predict bestsellers. Instead, it identifies books already demonstrating meaningful market activity and evaluates whether they also present meaningful publishing opportunity.

Every stage systematically removes noise until only a small set of meaningful publishing opportunities remains.

Process Overview

From broad market scan to focused opportunity list.

800+ New Releases → Objective Screening → Editorial Review → Metadata Collection → Opportunity Assessment → Front List

Research Process

Phase 1

Market Collection

At the beginning of each week, Front List Intel surveys more than 800 newly released titles across multiple publishing categories—from Biography & Memoir to Science Fiction & Fantasy—to establish the initial universe for analysis.

Phase 2

Objective Screening

The initial universe undergoes a consistent, rules-based screening process designed to remove obvious non-candidates. This process narrows the field to titles that may represent meaningful publishing opportunities.

Phase 3

Editorial Review

The remaining candidates undergo a publishing-focused editorial review designed to identify authors and titles that align with Front List Intel's scope and mission. This stage considers an author's commercial profile, body of work, market relevance, and potential publishing opportunity. The objective is to focus additional research on the most promising candidates while significantly reducing the candidate pool and keeping the focus on emerging opportunity rather than popularity alone.

Phase 4

Metadata Collection

The remaining candidates undergo a deeper level of research designed to build a more complete commercial profile for each title.

Additional metadata—including publication details, format availability, pricing, reader engagement, and other market indicators—is collected to provide the context necessary for evaluating potential publishing opportunities.

Phase 5

Opportunity Assessment

The remaining candidates undergo a final evaluation to identify meaningful publishing opportunities.

This assessment considers the complete commercial profile developed during earlier phases and identifies signals that may be relevant to publishing professionals, including format opportunities, publishing models, and other indicators of commercial opportunity.

By this stage, the initial universe has been reduced to a small number of titles demonstrating both meaningful market momentum and meaningful publishing opportunity.

Momentum Framework

Titles that appear on the Front List continue to be monitored each week using a proprietary momentum framework.

The Momentum Score is a proprietary score ranging from 0–100 that considers current Kindle Store Rank, week-over-week Kindle Store Rank movement, and week-over-week ratings growth.

The score helps determine whether a title is demonstrating Active Momentum, Sustained Momentum, or Cooling, allowing Front List Intel to monitor how a title's commercial trajectory changes over time.

The Momentum Score is designed to compare titles within the Front List and should not be interpreted as an absolute measure of commercial success.

The exact methodology, weighting, and classification thresholds are proprietary and intentionally not published.

Long-Term Validation

Front List Intel measures its methodology over time by comparing previously identified titles against major bestseller lists and other market outcomes.

As the historical dataset grows, those comparisons help validate whether emerging opportunities were identified before broader commercial recognition.

Inside the Report

Featured Sections

The report is organized into four sections: New Discoveries, Featured This Week, Continuing Coverage, and Newly Archived. Each section reflects a different stage in a title's commercial trajectory.

Book Links

Book covers and titles link directly to Amazon product pages, allowing readers to review descriptions, formats, pricing, and reader response.

Author Links

Author names are clickable and link to author profile pages, making it easier to review an author's catalog, publishing history, and related titles.

Title Profile

The Title Profile summarizes key commercial characteristics of the book, including format availability, Kindle Unlimited participation, series information, pricing, and publication details when available.

Author Profile

The Author Profile provides concise context about the author's publishing background, catalog depth, format history, and commercial position.

Observations

Observations combine market signals with publishing-specific context to explain why a title appears on the Front List and how its status is changing over time.
